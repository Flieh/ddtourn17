Diplomacy Rules:

Rules: The tournament will abide by all rules used by the Droidippy app.

Tournament Play:

Ranking: All players will be seeded with a rank of 1 through n (n being the number of players). Due to the fact that players are not ranked these seeds will be generated randomly.

Stages: The tournament will consist of two stages. The first stage will be a round robin format. After the round robin, the seven players with the highest overall scores will face off in a final stage.

Round Robin: Players will be assigned to games based upon their randomly assigned seeds. Players 1 through 7 will play in game one, and the rest of the games will be filled similarly. Efforts will be made to ensure that players play as many different players as possible.

All players shall play a minimum of 4 rounds, with the majority playing 5. The number of people who will play 4 rounds depends on the number of players who sign up for the tournament. Having a number of players that is not a multiple of 7 means that not everyone will be able to play the same number of games. As a result some players will have to play 4 rounds instead of 5. To make this as fair as possible, the last people to sign up for the tournament will play the four games. For example, if there are 50 players (one above a multiple of 7) 5 players will have to play 4 games to properly create a schedule.

Note: The way that this will be taken into account with regards to scoring can be seen in the tournament scoring section of the rules.

Final: The players with the top seven scores throughout the round robin will be selected to play in a final match. At the end of the final match, players’ scores will be added to their round robin scores. At this point, the player with the highest overall score will be considered to be the tournament champion.

NMR in the Final: In the event of a Spring ’01 NMR in the final game, the player ranked eighth in the round robin will be asked to play in the final. If the tournament director does not hear back from this player within 48 hours, the tournament director will take the seventh spot in the final match.

Game Scheduling: Players will receive their schedule on a round by round basis as the tournament goes on. A full schedule will not be given at the start of the tournament. Only players’ first round match-ups. This is to allow for players dropping out and joining after the tournament has started. To allow players to focus on first year negotiations, games will start one week after each other.

Forming Games: The tournament director will ask one player from each game to form a game. When the game has been formed and the code sent to the tournament director, it will be distributed to all other players in that game. In the event that a game is setup incorrectly (wrong end date, preference list not random, etc.) Players should contact the tournament director and inform them of the issue.

Country Assignments: Players will be assigned countries at random, using Droidippy’s game generator.

Gameplay: All games will be played with the standard Droidippy procedures.

Game Times: Games will have 36 hour deadlines.

Game Scoring: C-Diplo will be used to score this tournament. This is scored using the following.

  • 1 point each for playing (7)
  • 1 point for each supply center in your possession at the end of the game (34)
  • 38 points for topping the board (38)
  • 14 points for coming second (14)
  • 7 points for coming third (7)

A solo results in the winner obtaining 100 points and everybody else 0.

In the event of a tie the tied players shall share the points for the positions in which they were tied. For example, if two players come second, they will share the 14 points for second, and 7 points for third.

Game end: Games will end when the first of the following occurs;

  1. One player reaches a solo
  2. A draw is agreed upon by all players
  3. Year 1910 ends

Reporting Game Completion: After a game has been completed, the winner of the game is asked to email the tournament director with the results.

Tournament Scoring: To account for the variation in number of games played, only the best three scores of each player will be counted towards their overall tournament score.

Ties: In the event of a tie in the tournament standings, the player with the most of the following will be considered the victor;

  • Most Solos
  • Most Second Place Finishes
  • Most Third Place Finishes
  • Head to head (If the players played each other, the player with the better score shall be considered the victor.

Replacements: If an issue arises whereby a player can no longer continue playing the game, or a country has been in civil disorder for one full year; the tournament director will assign a replacement player to finish the game.

Replacement Players: The tournament director will attempt to find a player who is not participating in the tournament to fill empty countries. Failing this, the tournament director will finish the game as the empty country. If neither of these are possible, the tournament director will randomly choose a player in the tournament to fill the role. This player will not receive a score for the game in which they are a replacement.

Note: Players do not have any obligation to participate as a replacement. If the randomly selected player does not wish to play, the tournament director will ask someone else.

Absent Players: If a player has become unresponsive during one of their games, the tournament director will make an effort to contact them to determine the issue. If the tournament director is unable to make contact, they will disqualify them, and assign replacement players to the absentee’s games at the tournament director’s discretion.

Reinstatement: If an absent player responds to the tournament director after measures were taken to fill the player’s games, the player may be reinstated. However, this will be solely at the tournament director’s discretion.

Late joiners: Should players request to join the tournament after the registration deadline, they may have the ability to participate at the tournament director’s discretion. If they join before the end of the second round the tournament director may allow them to play the final three rounds and count these three scores. The tournament director may also keep these players as a spare if they join after the end of the second round, or if they deem it necessary for the good of the tournament. Should they be kept as spares, if they participate in three games as substitutions starting in the first year of play, their scores shall count as a full game.

Note: If for any reason a player is unable to continue in the tournament, please contact the tournament director, so that they might be able to better schedule the rest of the tournament.

Disputes and Misconduct:

Disputes: In the event a dispute arises between players, they are invited to solve it amongst themselves. If unable to do so, the tournament director will make a decision regarding the dispute. Any decision rendered by the tournament director will be considered binding and will not be subject to appeal.

Misconduct: Players are encouraged to conduct themselves in a proper manner, and any player who commits a misconduct will be penalized by the tournament director. All penalties laid out by the tournament director are final. There will be no appeal. If the tournament director deems the offense to be serious, they will forward the complaint to Our Lord of Diplomacy Chris Babcock. Misconducts include but are not limited to the following.

  • Entering the tournament multiple times
  • Breaking tournament rules
  • Verbal abuse of fellow players
  • Verbal abuse of tournament personnel
  • Use of racial slurs or other distasteful language
  • Deliberate disruption of the tournament
  • General assholeishness

Penalties: Penalties will be assessed by the tournament director and are comprised of but not limited to the following.

  • Point deduction
  • Disqualification
  • Written warnings
  • Ban from tournaments
  • Ban from the app (At Chris’ discretion)

Reporting Misconduct: To report any misconduct please contact the tournament director and explain the issue. If it is a report of an issue such as racial slurs please attach screenshots of the comments to the email. As the tournament is being run on the Droidippy app, these issues may also be of concern to Chris. As a result feel free to copy him on the email, or send it to him first. If the tournament director is the only one to receive the email they will forward it on to Chris if they deem it necessary.

Note: Droidippy is a game where people sometimes wish to act as if they are in the time period. This can result in period specific language that could be offensive. This will be taken into account when necessary.

Please note that the tournament director may rule on subjects not covered by the rules. They may decide to go against the rules if they determine that it would be for the overall good of the tournament.

Tournament Awards:

Tournament Winner: Awarded to the winner of the tournament

Player’s Choice: The player who is most fun to play with; as voted by other players

The Twitch: Awarded to the most unpredictable/indecisive player as voted by others.

The Invisible Blade: Awarded to the player most likely to stab you in the back. As voted on by other players.
